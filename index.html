My Journey

2020-present

Currently enrolled in the MSCS Align Program at Northeastern University. After completing the first year of the program, which is an accelerated, intensive adaptation of a undergraduate education in Computer Science, I am in the masters portion of the course. So far I have learned Python, Java, C, Object-Oriented Programming and Discrete Mathematics. Currently, I am learning Web Development using the MERN stack and Software Engineering.

2015-2019

I graduated with my Masters of Divinity (M.Div.) degree from Gordon-Conwell Theological Seminary. My education required me to learn how to read and translate the Christian Bible from it's original languages of Greek and Hebrew (sorry no Aramaic). I learned about the good and bad history of Christianity, and to appreciate the depth (and breadth) of the Christian tradition. I also was trained in how to work well with people. Here I learned the arts of listening, teamwork and walking with people through the highest of highs and the lowest of lows.

2012-2018

I worked as a Youth Pastor/Director at St. John's Korean United Methodist Church in Lexington, MA for a group of about 30-35 amazing young people (ages 11-18). Here I learned how to communicate, giving weekly messages, preparing lessons, offering religious counseling and trying to explain difficult concepts clearly. Here I learned the joy of being kind and patient. I got the chance to work on my emotional intelligence, help struggling communities through service trips and experienced the powerful reward of investing in the next generation. These were the 'good-old-days', at least so far. =)

2008-2012

I graduated with my bachelors degree in Philosophy with a senior thesis titled "All Form and No Content?: applications of Kant's second categorical imperative". During this time I learned how to think critically, analyze and appraise arguments. My particular interests were in ethics and the history of Philosphy, which I viewed, at least in part, as a series of iterations by different philosophers and systems to conceptualize reality.
